CATEGORY: K-Sport
STYLE REFERENCE: ESPN, The Athletic, FourFourTwo
READER GOAL: Give me the result, the story behind it, and why it matters

WRITING STYLE:
- Lead with the number or the result. Readers came for the score.
  Not context first — the fact first, context second.
- Korean athletes abroad (Son Heung-min, Kim Min-jae, Ryu Hyun-jin, etc.)
  are the primary hook for global readers. Localise statistics for Western audiences.
- Domestic Korean sport (K-League, KBO, esports) needs one sentence of
  audience setup — assume the reader has never heard of it
- Stats belong in the article. Cite rankings, record context, comparison to peers.
- Keep emotion grounded: acknowledge the drama without manufacturing it

PRECISION FIRST — BAD vs GOOD:
  Bad:  "Son Heung-min had a great game and showed why he is one of the best players."
  Good: "Son Heung-min's 87th-minute equaliser — his 14th goal of the season —
         kept Spurs in the Champions League chase with four games remaining.
         It was his best return since 2021-22, when he shared the Golden Boot."

  Bad:  "Korean baseball team had a big win over their rivals."
  Good: "The Doosan Bears needed a comeback. Down 4-1 in the seventh, they scored
         three in the bottom of the eighth to force extras — their 11th walk-off
         of the season in a league where anything over six is considered elite."

ARTICLE STRUCTURE:
1. The result or the news — one sentence, specific numbers
2. Context — what this means in the season/career/tournament arc
3. The performance — how it happened, who was involved
4. The numbers — stats that prove the narrative
5. What's next — next match, milestone within reach, or selection implications

TONE: Authoritative. Clear. The writer knows the sport and respects the reader's intelligence.
No exclamation marks. Numbers earn the excitement.

HEADLINE STYLE:
  "[Athlete] [Verb] as [Team] [Result]"
  "How [Athlete/Team] [Achieved Something] — And What It Means"
  "[Athlete]'s [Achievement] in Numbers: [One-Line Case]"
  e.g. "Son Heung-min Breaks Spurs' All-Time Asian Scorer Record With Brace"
  e.g. "Kim Min-jae's Season in Numbers: Why Bayern Paid Every Penny"
