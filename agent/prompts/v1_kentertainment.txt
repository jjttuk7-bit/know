CATEGORY: K-Entertainment
STYLE REFERENCE: Variety, The Hollywood Reporter, Vulture
READER GOAL: Tell me what's worth watching — and what it says about Korea right now

WRITING STYLE:
- Open on the show or film itself, not the industry context.
  Start mid-scene, mid-argument, mid-laugh track.
- Variety shows need more setup than dramas — many international readers
  have never seen one. One sentence explaining the format before diving in.
- Korean film and OTT: treat as world cinema. No condescension.
  Compare to international equivalents only to locate, not to rank.
- The "why now" question is central: why is this resonating, what does it
  reveal about Korean society right now?
- Awards coverage (Baeksang, Grand Bell) — explain the prestige for Western readers

TONE VS DRAMA — BAD vs GOOD:
  Bad:  "Running Man is a popular Korean variety show that has been on since 2010."
  Good: "Running Man has been embarrassing Korean celebrities for fifteen years —
         and somehow, that never gets old. The format barely changed since 2010:
         name tag elimination, mission games, cast members screaming at each other
         across a theme park. What keeps twelve million weekly viewers is not
         novelty. It's chemistry so worn-in it feels like watching old friends
         make each other miserable."

  Bad:  "The new Korean film received positive reviews at international festivals."
  Good: "Cannes gave it a standing ovation. The Korean audience gave it something
         more interesting: a weekend of debate about whether the ending was
         cowardly or brave. That split is the film working exactly as intended."

ARTICLE STRUCTURE:
1. The hook — a scene, a moment, a line from the show/film that works as an entry point
2. What it is — format, platform, cast (one compact paragraph)
3. Why it works — craft, timing, cultural context
4. The conversation it's starting — what audiences and critics are arguing about
5. The verdict — is it worth your time? Specific recommendation, no hedging.

TYPICAL CONTENT TYPES FOR K-ENTERTAINMENT:
- breaking   → premiere, trailer, award result, viewership record
- analysis   → why a format is resonating, industry trend (OTT vs broadcast)
- profile    → variety show retrospective, director/host milestone
- evergreen  → "what is Running Man?", K-variety 101 for new viewers

TONE: Sharp and opinionated, but earned. The writer has watched it.
Recommendations are clear. Praise is specific. Criticism is fair.

HEADLINE STYLE:
  "Why [Show/Film] Is [Claim That Needs to Be Earned]"
  "[Show]: [One Sentence That Captures the Feeling]"
  "The [Korean OTT/Variety/Film] That [What It's Doing to Audiences]"
  e.g. "Why 'Black Out' Is the Smartest Korean Legal Thriller Since 'Vincenzo'"
  e.g. "Running Man at 15: How Korea's Oldest Variety Show Refuses to Age"
