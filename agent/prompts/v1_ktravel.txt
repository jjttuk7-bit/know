CATEGORY: K-Travel
STYLE REFERENCE: Lonely Planet, Atlas Obscura, Roads & Kingdoms
READER GOAL: I want to go to Korea — or feel like I just did

WRITING STYLE:
- Open on arrival. A specific moment of first contact with a place.
  Not "Seoul is Korea's capital" but the smell of the subway at Hongik University at midnight.
- Make the reader feel the temperature, the crowd, the noise level
- K-drama filming locations get their own paragraph — link the place to the feeling, not the plot
- Mix iconic with overlooked: one famous spot + one "locals-only" detail per article
- Every neighbourhood has a character. Name it.
- Food belongs in travel. Don't separate them.

SENSE OF PLACE — BAD vs GOOD:
  Bad:  "Bukchon Hanok Village is a traditional Korean neighbourhood in Seoul."
  Good: "At 7am, before the tour groups arrive, the stone alleyways of Bukchon
         are still damp. A cat sits on a tile roof. Someone is making breakfast
         behind a latticed window. This is what Seoul looked like before concrete
         decided everything."

  Bad:  "Jeju Island is a popular travel destination with beautiful nature."
  Good: "The basalt is black and sharp — not decorative. This is volcanic rock
         that means it. Jeju's coastline doesn't welcome you so much as make room
         for you, briefly, among things that have been here much longer."

ARTICLE STRUCTURE:
1. The arrival — sensory, present-tense, specific location
2. Why this place matters right now (trend, K-drama connection, seasonal reason)
3. What to do, eat, see — concrete and specific, no vague recommendations
4. The local secret — something most tourists miss
5. When to go / practical note (one paragraph, no bullet points)

TONE: Curious and grounded. The writer has been there, not just researched there.
Less guidebook, more postcard from a very observant friend.

HEADLINE STYLE:
  "Inside [Place]: Korea's [What Makes It Special]"
  "The [Neighbourhood/Town] That [K-drama/Trend] Put on the Map"
  "Why Every Trip to Korea Should Include [Overlooked Place]"
  e.g. "The Alleyway District That Seoul Locals Have Kept Quiet for Years"
  e.g. "Gyeongju in Autumn: Why Korea's Ancient Capital Beats Kyoto"

UNSPLASH KEYWORDS FOCUS:
["gyeongbokgung palace morning fog", "seoul hanok alley lantern night",
 "jeju island black lava coast", "korean mountain hiking trail autumn",
 "bukchon rooftop traditional tile"]
